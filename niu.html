Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map
About the project

The Hungarian National Innovation Agency previously had a basic, early-stage user interface for their Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map. They launched a call to redesign the site, aiming to make it more intuitive, interactive, and enjoyable to use. Our team, consisting of Anna Nina Tötő, Luca Sára Kiss, and myself, took on the challenge and collaborated to create a more engaging and user-friendly design.

 

UX design
UI design
Probem & Pain Points
While reviewing the old map design, we found several usability issues that made navigation confusing. To guide the new concept, we identified key patterns to avoid.
  • Clickable and non-clickable elements looking the same
  • Poor information hierarchy and grouping
  • Information overload, making it hard to find relevant content and connections
  • Intrusive, unhelpful popups
Our goal was to replace popups with on-screen content, using smooth, engaging interactions to create a seamless and enjoyable map experience.
During the redesign process, we aimed to create a transparent, aesthetically modern, and intuitive user experience, where visual appeal and content emphasis seamlessly intertwine.
Spacious layout: Improved readability with less information overload and a clean, uncluttered feel.
Icons: Help users understand content more quickly and intuitively through visual cues.
Hierarchy and consistency: Visually distinct, well-structured sections make indicators and focus areas easier to grasp at a glance.
My role - Services and funding opportunities

I was responsible for designing the Services and Funding Opportunities section. The main challenge was to create an intuitive filtering system for various interest areas, each with its own set of levels. These levels often overlapped, which made it tricky to present relevant opportunities clearly without overwhelming the user.

 

To address the overlaps, I designed a color and size hierarchy system that visually highlights which funding opportunities are most widely available across multiple interest areas. This helped users quickly understand relevance and availability at a glance.

Color and size hierarchy:

The brighter and larger a box is, the more relevant it is.

Visual filtering:

Instead of a dropdown menu, I used an interactive solution.

Animations:

Smooth, attention-grabbing transitions enhance the experience.

Dynamic UI:

Instead of pop-up windows, I used boxes that change in size and content.
